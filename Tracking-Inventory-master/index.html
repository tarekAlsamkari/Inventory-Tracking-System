WHAT WE DO ?

Tracking system

By using a tracking system, carriers know exactly where all vehicles of their fleet are. The black box, which is integrated into the vehicle, is at the heart of the tracking system. GPRS data communication provides the back office with real-time information on when and where a truck stops, for how long it remains on a place, and when it leaves and what route it takes.

Person tracking

Person tracking relies on unique identifiers that are temporally (RFID tags) or permanently assigned to persons like personal identifiers (including biometric identifiers), or national identification numbers and a way to sample their positions, either on short temporal scales as through GPS or for public administration to keep track of a state's citizens or temporary residents.

TRACKING SYSTEM BENEFITS

An advanced tracking system allows carriers to automatically process data, such as waiting, driving, resting and service times. Based on these data, they can optimise the logistics planning, payroll administration and transparency when collaborating with subcontractors, as the tracking data are stored. the back office has an archive with histories of the routes travelled.
